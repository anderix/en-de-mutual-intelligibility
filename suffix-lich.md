# Suffix -lich

The German *-lich* suffix attaches to nouns or verbs to make adjectives or adverbs. It maps reliably to English *-ly* (the same Old English ending that became *-ly*). In some cases it maps to English *-ish* or *-ful*.

The etymological connection: both German *-lich* and English *-ly* descend from Proto-Germanic */-līkaz/*, originally meaning "having the body of" or "having the form of."

## Pairs

**lucky / happy ↔ glücklich** *(A1, from Glück)*
*Nach der Prüfung war ich sehr glücklich.*

**heartily / cordially ↔ herzlich** *(A1, from Herz)*
*Wir begrüßen dich herzlich bei uns.*

**masculine / male ↔ männlich** *(A1, from Mann)*
*Bitte wählen Sie: männlich oder weiblich.*

**possible ↔ möglich** *(A1, from mögen)*
The English equivalent uses Latin-derived *-ible*; the German uses native *-lich*. Both derive from the same semantics.
*Ist es möglich, früher zu kommen?*

**punctual ↔ pünktlich** *(A1, from Punkt)*
*Der Zug fährt pünktlich um neun Uhr ab.*

**feminine / female ↔ weiblich** *(A1, from Weib, archaic for woman)*
*Das Formular fragt nach männlich oder weiblich.*

## Pairs to expect at higher levels

*freundlich* (friendly), *täglich* (daily), *wöchentlich* (weekly), *monatlich* (monthly), *jährlich* (yearly), *natürlich* (naturally), *wirklich* (really), *plötzlich* (suddenly), *peinlich* (embarrassing/painful), *gemütlich* (cozy — famous untranslatable), *gefährlich* (dangerous), *wichtig* (important — uses *-ig* instead).

## Note

When you see a German adjective ending in *-lich*, mentally substitute *-ly* and look for the root noun. *Herz + lich = heart-ly = heartily*. *Glück + lich = luck-ly = lucky*. *Punkt + lich = point-ly = punctual* (slight semantic stretch). The mental substitution gets you to a recognizable English form most of the time.
