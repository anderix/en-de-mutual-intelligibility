# Suffix -tion

The Latin *-tion* suffix is shared identically between English and German. Both languages inherited it from Latin via French, and the spelling stayed exactly the same. Only the pronunciation differs (see note below).

These nouns are always feminine in German (*die*).

## Pairs

**information ↔ die Information** *(A1)*
*Ich suche Informationen über den Kurs.*

**reception ↔ die Rezeption** *(A1)*
Spelling differs slightly: English *reception*, German *Rezeption* (note the *z* for the /ts/ sound).
*Die Rezeption öffnet um sechs Uhr.*

**international ↔ international** *(A1, adjective form)*
The adjective form is also identically spelled.
*Das Festival ist international bekannt.*

**portion ↔ die Portion** *(A2)*
*Eine kleine Portion Pommes, bitte.*

**situation ↔ die Situation** *(A2)*
*Wir sind in einer schwierigen Situation.*

## Pairs to expect at higher levels

Massive yield above A1: *Nation / nation*, *Station / station*, *Position / position*, *Konversation / conversation*, *Reservation / reservation*, *Generation / generation*, *Operation / operation*, *Demonstration / demonstration*, *Dokumentation / documentation*, *Konzentration / concentration*, *Inflation / inflation*, *Diskussion / discussion* (same family with *-ssion*), *Mission / mission*, *Vision / vision*.

## Pronunciation difference (spelled-identical, sound-different)

Spelled identically, pronounced very differently:
- English *information* → "in-fer-MAY-shun" (the *-tion* is /ʃən/)
- German *Information* → "in-for-mah-tsi-OHN" (the *-tion* is /tsi-ohn/)

The German pronunciation is fully syllabic: *-ti-on* is two syllables, with stress on the final syllable. This is the consistent rule for all *-tion* words in German. Once you hear it, you hear it everywhere.
